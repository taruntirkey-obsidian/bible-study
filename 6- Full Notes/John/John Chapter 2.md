### The Wedding at Cana
**Day 7** [^1]  marks a significant event where Jesus attends a wedding feast in Cana with His disciples (**John 2:2-3**). Mary, the Mother of Jesus, is present (**John 2:1**). Notably, John mentions her only twice in his Gospel: at the beginning of Jesus' public ministry and at the crucifixion (**John 19:25-27**). In both instances, Jesus addresses her as "Woman," alluding to her role as the new Eve, mother of the living (**Genesis 3:15, 20**). This typology highlights John's theological perspective.

Six stone jars for ablution are present, each holding 20-30 gallons (75.7-113.56 liters). When Mary informs Jesus that the wine has run out, He responds by providing an abundance of wine. This event can be seen as the fulfillment of the Old Law (represented by the wine that ran out) being replaced by the New Law (represented by the wine Jesus provides). Jesus, in this context, can be seen as the completion or perfection of the Old Law, laying the foundation for His Church.

The number 7 in Jewish culture signifies completion, perfection, and holiness. This theme is echoed in Genesis, where God created everything in 7 days and blessed the 7th day, making it holy (**Genesis 2:3**). Jesus' presence and first miracle at the wedding, initiating His public ministry, can be seen as a new creation. Some interpretations suggest that Adam and Eve were married by God on the 7th day, adding another layer of significance to this event.

Moreover, Jesus' teaching on the Sabbath (**Mark 2:27**) reveals that it was created for humans, highlighting God's intention for humanity's benefit. This underscores Jesus' role in fulfilling and transforming the Old Covenant.
### Cleansing of the Temple
Jesus demonstrated His authority as the Son of God by cleansing the Temple. When asked for a sign by the Jews, He replied, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up" (**John 2:19**). This enigmatic statement pointed to His own death and resurrection, hinting that His resurrected Body would be the true Temple, where the fullness of God's presence dwells (**Colossians 2:9**). Through His sacrifice and resurrection, Jesus would establish a new mode of worship, where believers, united with Him as His mystical Body, the Church, would offer true worship to God in and through Him.
