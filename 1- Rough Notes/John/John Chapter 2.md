### The Wedding at Cana
Day 7 [^1] - Jesus attends the wedding feast in Cana with His disciples where they were invited. (2-3)

Mary, the Mother of Jesus was there (John 2:1). John in his Gospel mentions her only twice, once at the time of beginning of Jesus' public ministry and at the crucifixion (John 19:25-27). And in both of the mentioned events, Jesus called His mother as "Women", alluding to her as the new Eve, mother of the living (Genesis 3:15,20). John is putting a theological perspective here.

6 stone jars for ablution were present there, each could hold 20-30 gallons (75.7-113.56 liters). When Mother Mary informed Jesus that wine has run out and then Jesus doing His first miracle, John could be pointing out the beginning of the fulfillment the Old Law (represented by wine which ran out) by the New Law (represented by the wine which Jesus gives through his first miracle) with Jesus being the 7th stone jar signifying the completion of the Old Law laying the foundation for His Church. The number 7 in Jewish culture represents completion, perfection and holiness.
### Cleansing of the Temple
Jesus showed his authority as the Son of God by cleaning the Temple. On being asked for a sign by the Jews, He tells that He will raise a new Temple for worship, alluding to His resurrected Body as the new Temple where we will offer God true worship in, with and through Him.
### Points to Reflect
- In Genesis, God created everything in 7 days and blessed the 7th day and made it holy. (Genesis 2:3).
- Jesus blessed the wedding with His presence and His first miracle initiating His public ministry. (2, 11)
- This structure highlights John's deliberate connection between Jesus' ministry and the creation narrative, emphasizing Jesus' role in the new creation.
- According to some interpretations and theological perspective of some Bible scholars, Adam and Eve were married by God on the 7th day.
- Interestingly, Jesus who is the 2nd person in the Holy Trinity reveals that the Sabbath was created for humans not the other way around (Mark 2:27)
